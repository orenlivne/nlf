We submit for consideration the manuscript \textbf{``NLF: A Resistor-Network Framework and
Linear-Time Solver for Convex Network-Flow Equilibria''} by Oren E.\ Livne, for publication in
\textit{SIAM Journal on Scientific Computing} in the section
\textbf{Numerical Algorithms for Scientific Computing}.

The paper presents NLF, a unified solver for a broad class of convex network-flow problems ---
congestion (traffic) routing, minimum-delay communication routing, and exact maximum flow ---
cast as a single nonlinear graph Laplacian $B\rho(B^{\top}\phi)=\alpha d$.
The solver combines a damped chord-Newton iteration on a frozen linearization with
pseudo-arclength continuation through the max-flow fold, delegating each linear step to a
near-linear Laplacian solver (approximate Cholesky default; LAMG+ interchangeable).
The whole nonlinear solve costs 2--4 linear Laplacian solves, making the wall-clock empirically
$\mathcal{O}(m)$ in the edge count across a 2,003-graph SuiteSparse corpus spanning five size
decades (up to $5.6\times10^7$ edges).
Against a state-of-the-art interior-point method, NLF is a median $2.6\times$ faster where both
converge and $>45\times$ on poorly-separable graphs where the IPM's direct factorization is
superlinear; against matrix-free L-BFGS, a median $4.2\times$ faster and the only solver to
complete within budget on the 90 hardest instances.

We believe this work is well matched to SISC's scope: it introduces a practical, reproducible
method with empirical near-linear complexity, a large-scale corpus evaluation, and a unifying
framework across three classical problem classes.
Code, scripts, and benchmark data are openly available at
\url{https://github.com/orenlivne/nlf}.
We request the \textbf{SISC Reproducibility Badge: code and data available}.

This manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere and is not under review at another journal.
All authors have approved submission.
